About me

I am an experienced data scientist, data engineer, and software developer looking to continue my career in solving complex data problems using programming, machine learning, and statistics.

I have a proven record of work success in fast-paced R&D environments. A lot of my experience of working with data and programming comes from my PhD work in particle physics at the Large Hadron Collider, where I developed and implemented pipelines for data processing, machine learning, and statistical analysis in C++ and Python. I have more than 10 year of programming experience in these languages. My research was on one of the flagship data analysis projects at CERN, and was the measurement of the Higgs boson production cross section in the diphoton decay channel [cds: 2766569]. My PhD work also involved working with particle detector electronics, as well as processing data from sensors and image-like data. As part of the detector data analysis, I applied geometric clustering algorithms.

After defending my PhD in physics, I decided to pursue a career in data science, because I felt that my passion lies closer to working with data and developing software for that purpose. I have industry experience in data science from working at Revelio Labs in 2022, where I worked on data-driven models, which I engineered and optimized for both quality of predictions and computation speed. At Revelio Labs, I worked extensively with large SQL databases, specifically using AWS and Snowflake.

I have experience from both industry and academic settings of working under stringent time constraints to meet project deadlines, and also of documenting my research and software. I also have a passion for building software tools that can be applied to recurring problems. On a number of occasions, I developed tools, which I successfully shared with colleagues, and which helped automate and speed up data analysis procedures and pipelines.

I'm excited about working on the challenging problems, requiring experience and expertise in multiple areas to converge on a solution.
